Scottish Rain

It doesn't fall here, not exactly —
it arrives, sideways and certain,
as if it owns the place.

The hills have learned to wear it
like a second skin,
grey-green and glistening,
the thistle bowed but holding.

In Edinburgh it finds the closes,
threads between the tenements,
pools in the cobblestones
that kings once crossed.

On Skye it is a curtain
drawn across the Cuillins,
then pulled back without warning
to show you what you missed.

It tastes of peat and altitude,
of lochs that swallow light.
It is not weather here —
it is the place itself, arriving.

And still the people walk through it,
hoods down, faces up,
because in Scotland
you do not wait for the rain to stop.
